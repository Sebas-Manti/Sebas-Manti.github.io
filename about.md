I'm finishing a mathematics degree while working as a data engineer; which means I spend my days inside enterprise data systems and my evenings trying to make sense of equations that are, formally, undefined.
My mathematical interests center on stochastic partial differential equations: problems where randomness and continuous fields interact in ways that break classical intuition, where you sometimes need to build new language just to state what a solution should mean. I'm also interested in high-performance simulation and have a paper in computational geometry.
When I'm not doing math I make videos, read essays, ask questions that don't have clean answers, and walk around Bogotá on the days it decides to be sunny.

## Background

My interest is in the theoretical side of stochastic PDEs, understanding why equations driven by rough noise require entirely new tools to even state rigorously, and what it means to "solve" something that classical analysis can't handle. I'm currently building the foundations: measure-theoretic probability, functional analysis, and stochastic processes, working through Çınlar and Geiss alongside the PDE literature. I'm early enough in this that I'm more certain about the questions than where they'll take me.
