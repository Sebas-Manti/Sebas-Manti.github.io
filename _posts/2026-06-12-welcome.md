This site is where I write about mathematics, share things I find interesting, and document some of my projects.

Most of the writing will be about **stochastic partial differential equations** — things like singular SPDEs, regularity structures, the KPZ equation, and related topics. Occasionally I'll wander elsewhere.

A short example to confirm that $\LaTeX$ renders correctly. The **stochastic heat equation** on $\mathbb{R}$ is

$$\partial_t u = \tfrac{1}{2} \partial_x^2 u + \xi,$$

where $\xi$ is space-time white noise. Already with this innocent-looking equation there are subtleties: $u(t,x)$ is only a distribution in $x$, and any nonlinear function of $u$ requires care to define. The multiplicative version

$$\partial_t u = \tfrac{1}{2} \partial_x^2 u + u \cdot \xi$$

requires Itô's formula and Wick renormalization to make sense rigorously.
