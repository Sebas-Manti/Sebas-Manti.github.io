This is one of the best public expositions of what singular SPDEs are actually about and why they require new mathematics. Hairer explains the core difficulty — that solutions to equations driven by rough noise cannot be understood by classical methods — and gives an accessible account of the renormalization ideas behind **regularity structures**.

The talk is aimed at a general mathematical audience, so it's a good starting point before looking at the technical papers. Hairer received the Fields Medal in 2014 for this work.

**Related reading:** The ICM 2014 proceedings paper *[Singular stochastic PDEs](https://www.hairer.org/papers/ProceedingsICM.pdf)* is a more technical but still very readable overview.
